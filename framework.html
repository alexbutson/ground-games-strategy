Strategic Impact Realization Framework

One framework, from individual intent to enterprise governance.

Process can be designed. Technology can be deployed. But until you build a self-reinforcing system that aligns people, measurement, and accountability to the outcomes that matter, you are managing symptoms — not causes. SIRF is that system.

Why strategy stalls

Three forces keep capable organizations stuck

Value displacement

Unplanned, low-value work crowds out planned, high-value work. Organizations rarely say no — every new "yes" silently displaces what was already committed, until reactive work becomes the system.

Frozen value

The capacity to perform is already inside your teams. Misalignment and unclear priorities keep it locked. Underperformance is usually an alignment gap, not a resource gap.

No translation layer

There is strategy at the top and activity at the bottom, with nothing reliable in between. Decisions degrade with every organizational layer they pass through.

The components

The SIRF operating model

Seven connected components take an organization from strategic intent all the way to governed, measurable delivery.

IVITranslate

Intent, Value & Impact

Strategic clarity has to exist at the individual and functional level — not just the executive tier — before any execution system can work. IVI is the translation layer that lets every team leader state, in one sentence, how their work advances the top priority.

MAPNavigate

The Strategy Map

A strategy map is a navigation tool, not a communication slide. SIRF treats the cause-and-effect relationships between objectives as the operating logic of the organization — telling you not just what to achieve, but in what order, through what mechanisms, and with what dependencies.

MEASMeasure

Measurement Architecture

Most organizations measure activity, not impact. SIRF balances leading and lagging indicators, quantitative and qualitative measures, operational and strategic metrics — as one complete system rather than a collection of dashboards. Every number traces to the stakeholder whose life it represents.

LAMPAlign

Leadership & Management Performance

Alignment is not a condition you achieve — it's a practice you sustain. LAMP separates the roles: executives set direction and remove structural barriers; managers translate strategy into daily decisions. Where those roles blur, alignment breaks down.

SIREExecute

Strategy Impact Realization Execution

A five-phase methodology for cascading enterprise strategy into functional execution without losing coherence: functional contribution mapping, cross-functional dependency management, initiative prioritization, and the governance that keeps execution honest.

GOVCalibrate

The Governance of Impact

Governance is the feedback loop that keeps strategy and execution calibrated to each other — review cadences, decision rights, escalation paths, and a Strategy Management Office. Most governance failures are failures of executive commitment, not process design.

CCCSustain

Capability, Capacity & Culture

Three conditions determine whether any execution system can sustain itself: capability (the skills to execute), capacity (the bandwidth to focus), and culture (an environment that reinforces the outcomes you want). Every engagement is grounded in an honest read of all three.
